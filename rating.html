Chess Rating

This first standard rating systems only started to emerge in the 20th century. A chess rating is a tool that makes it easy to measure a player's probable performance against another opponent. It is expressed by a number that can go from 100 to (theoretically) infinity.

While there are many different rating systems, the most popular one is the ELO system, used my FIDE (World Chess Federation).

Some Interesting Chess Rating Facts

  • The person who became the youngest chess grandmaster is Sergey Karjakin Ukraine who became a grandmaster at 12 years, 7 months, 0 days.
  • The peak chess rating ever achived by a person was 2882 by Magnus Carlsen in 2014.
  • The rating of chess engines are much higher than that of humans with Stockfish having an estimated rating of 3600
